Description of the data

This bar chart illustrates the percentage of students employed across different age ranges. The data shows that 39.9% of students in the 16-24 age group are employed, the lowest of the four groups. The employment rate increases to 47.0% for students aged 25-29, making this the highest employment rate among the age ranges. In the 30-39 age group, 45.4% of students are employed, which is slightly lower than the 25-29 group but still a significant proportion. The 40-49 age group has an employment rate of 43.6%, which is just below the 30-39 group. Overall, the employment rates for students in these age ranges fall within a narrow band of 39% to 47%, suggesting a relatively stable trend in student employment across these groups. The chart highlights that while employment rates tend to be highest for students aged 25-29, there is no dramatic drop-off in employment as students get older, with all age groups showing notable employment levels.
